Patient and Caregiver Stories

Robert Miller

Caregiver for his mother with Alzheimer's

After my mother's early diagnosis, we were able to plan ahead and make important decisions together while she could still participate fully in the process.

When my mother started showing subtle signs of memory loss, I was concerned but unsure what to do. A friend recommended the AlzPredict tool, which suggested she might be in the early stages of Alzheimer's. We immediately consulted with a neurologist who confirmed the diagnosis.

The early detection made all the difference. We had time to discuss her care preferences, organize her legal and financial affairs, and explore treatment options that have helped slow the progression. Most importantly, my mother was able to be an active participant in these decisions.

Three years later, she's still living at home with support, enjoying her garden and visits from her grandchildren. While we know what the future holds, the early diagnosis gave us precious time to prepare and make the most of every day.

Shared on June 15, 2023

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Neurologist

The early detection through this tool allowed us to start cognitive therapies and medication that significantly slowed the progression of symptoms in my patients.

As a neurologist specializing in neurodegenerative disorders, I've seen firsthand how early intervention can change the trajectory of Alzheimer's disease. The AlzPredict tool has become an invaluable resource in my practice.

One particular case stands out: a 67-year-old professor who used the tool after noticing minor difficulties with word recall. The prediction indicated early-stage Alzheimer's, which we confirmed through clinical assessment. We immediately started him on a comprehensive treatment plan including medication, cognitive exercises, lifestyle modifications, and regular monitoring.

Two years later, his cognitive decline has been much slower than typically expected. He's still teaching part-time and has maintained his independence. This case exemplifies why I recommend early screening tools like AlzPredict to all my at-risk patients.

Michael and Susan Thompson

Patient and Spouse

Knowledge is power. Early detection gave us time to prepare, adjust, and make the most of our retirement years together.

When Michael retired at 65, we had big plans for travel and spending time with our grandchildren. During a routine health check, his doctor suggested using the AlzPredict tool as a preventive measure due to his family history of dementia.

The results indicated mild cognitive impairment with a high risk of progression to Alzheimer's. After further medical evaluation confirmed these findings, we were initially devastated. However, we quickly realized that having this information early was actually a gift.

We adjusted our retirement plans, prioritizing the trips we most wanted to take while Michael was still able to fully enjoy them. We moved to a single-level home closer to our children and set up legal and financial arrangements for the future.

Michael started medication and cognitive therapy immediately, and three years later, his symptoms have progressed much more slowly than expected. We're still traveling, though we've modified how we do it, and we're making beautiful memories with our family every day.

Shared on August 22, 2023
